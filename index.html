Air Quality Index (AQI)

An Air Quality Index (AQI) is a number used by government agencies to measure the air pollution levels and communicate it to the population.
As the AQI increases, it means that a large percentage of the population will experience severe adverse health effects.
The measurement of the AQI requires an air monitor and an air pollutant concentration over a specified averaging period.

Solutions to Air Pollution

Addressing air pollution through engineering solutions involves implementing emission control technologies, promoting electric vehicles and sustainable transportation, transitioning to renewable energy sources, and adopting green building practices. Smart urban planning and effective waste management are also crucial. A multi-pronged approach, combining these engineering solutions with supportive policies and public engagement, is vital in reducing air pollution and safeguarding air quality for future generations.

Health Awareness

From smog hanging over cities to smoke inside the home, air pollution poses a major threat to health and climate. Ambient (outdoor) air pollution in both cities and rural areas is causing fine particulate matter which result in strokes, heart diseases, lung cancer, acute and chronic respiratory diseases.

Acts related to Air as a natural resource.
